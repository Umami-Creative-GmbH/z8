## Understanding Time Regulations

Time regulations define labor law compliance rules for your organization, including:
- Maximum daily working hours
- Required break durations
- Rest period requirements
- Overtime thresholds

---

## Creating Time Regulations

To set up time regulation rules:

1. Go to **Settings** → **Time Regulations**
2. Click **"Create Regulation"**
3. Configure the regulation:
   - **Name**: Descriptive name (e.g., "Germany Standard")
   - **Max Daily Hours**: Maximum hours per day (e.g., 10)
   - **Max Weekly Hours**: Maximum hours per week (e.g., 48)
   - **Break Rules**: Required breaks based on hours worked
4. Click **"Save"**

---

## Break Rules

Configure break requirements based on hours worked:

| Hours Worked | Required Break |
|--------------|----------------|
| > 6 hours | 30 minutes |
| > 9 hours | 45 minutes |

To add a break rule:
1. In the regulation editor, click **"Add Break Rule"**
2. Set the threshold (hours worked)
3. Set the required break duration
4. Save the regulation

---

## Importing Presets

Import pre-configured regulations based on country labor laws:

1. Go to **Settings** → **Time Regulations**
2. Click **"Import Preset"**
3. Select the country/region (e.g., Germany, Austria)
4. Review the imported rules
5. Click **"Import"**

Available presets include German ArbZG (Working Hours Act) and other European labor law templates.

---

## Assigning Regulations to Employees

Regulations can be assigned at multiple levels:

**Organization-wide:**
1. Go to **Settings** → **Time Regulations**
2. Set as organization default
3. Applies to all employees without specific assignment

**Team-level:**
1. Go to team settings
2. Assign a time regulation
3. Applies to all team members

**Individual:**
1. Go to employee profile
2. Assign a specific regulation
3. Overrides team/org defaults

---

## Compliance Monitoring

View compliance status for your organization:

1. Go to **Settings** → **Time Regulations** → **"Compliance"**
2. See violations and warnings:
   - Overtime violations
   - Missing breaks
   - Rest period violations
3. Export compliance reports for auditing
